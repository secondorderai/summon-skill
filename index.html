Why generic AI agents are not enough

A backend template that assumes Express is not much help inside a Hono + Drizzle + Cloudflare Workers repo. Summon starts from your codebase, not from a generic persona library.

How Summon works

Summon turns a repo into a project-native AI team through a lightweight workflow that stays inspectable, file-based, and versionable.

1

Scan the project

Detect frameworks, ORMs, databases, infrastructure, CI/CD, and local conventions directly from the repo.

2

Interview progressively

Confirm what was detected and ask targeted questions about error handling, testing philosophy, workflow, and pain points.

3

Generate specialist agents

Create role-specific agent files in .claude/agents/ with identity, mission, critical rules, stack context, workflow, and success metrics.

4

Compound team intelligence

Store patterns, anti-patterns, decisions, and learnings in .claude/evolution/ so future work benefits from past work.

What gets generated

Summon keeps the output simple and transparent: agent markdown files plus a shared evolution directory.

Generated structure

.claude/
├── agents/
│   ├── engineering-backend-engineer.md
│   ├── quality-security-auditor.md
│   ├── quality-code-reviewer.md
│   └── orchestrator.md
└── evolution/
    ├── patterns.md
    ├── anti-patterns.md
    ├── decisions.md
    └── learnings.md

Inside each agent

  • 1
    Identity & context
    Role, expertise, and communication style.
  • 2
    Core mission
    What this agent exists to do in concrete terms.
  • 3
    Critical rules
    Non-negotiable guardrails with rationale.
  • 4
    Stack context
    Your specific technologies, patterns, and constraints.
  • 5
    Evolution workflow
    How the agent learns from outcomes and proposes updates.
